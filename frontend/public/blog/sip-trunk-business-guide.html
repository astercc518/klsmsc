SIP Trunk for Business: Complete Setup Guide, Costs & Provider Selection 2025

📅 May 2025⏱ 12 min read🏷 SIP Trunk · VoIP · PBX · Business Calls

SIP trunking lets businesses replace expensive PSTN lines with internet-based voice, cutting calling costs by 40–70% while gaining flexibility that traditional phone lines can't match. Here's everything you need to know.

What Is SIP Trunking?

SIP (Session Initiation Protocol) trunking replaces traditional PSTN telephone lines with a virtual connection to the public telephone network via your internet connection. Instead of copper lines running into your building, a SIP trunk is a logical channel over IP that carries voice calls to and from the PSTN.

Think of it as a "virtual phone line" — you can have dozens or hundreds of simultaneous calls on a single internet connection, pay only for what you use, and manage everything from software rather than physical hardware.

How SIP Trunking Works

  1. Your IP PBX (Asterisk, FreePBX, 3CX, Cisco CUCM) connects to your SIP trunk provider over the internet
  2. Outbound calls: PBX sends the call over the SIP trunk to the provider → provider routes to PSTN → reaches the destination
  3. Inbound calls: Caller dials your number → hits PSTN → routes to your SIP trunk provider → delivers to your PBX over SIP
  4. Your PBX handles all internal routing — extensions, IVR, call queues, voicemail

Prerequisites

  • An IP PBX system (Asterisk/FreePBX, 3CX, Cisco, Avaya, etc.)
  • A stable internet connection with sufficient bandwidth (1 simultaneous call ≈ 85 kbps with G.711 codec)
  • A SIP trunk provider account with credentials
  • Inbound DID numbers for your locations

SIP Trunk Pricing Models

Pay-Per-Minute (Metered)

Best for businesses with variable call volumes. You pay only for minutes used, with per-minute rates typically $0.005–$0.030 depending on destination. No channel fees — scale to as many simultaneous calls as needed (within your agreed limits).

Channel-Based (Flat Rate)

Pay a monthly fee per concurrent call channel regardless of usage. Best for high-volume businesses. Typically $5–$20/channel/month with included minutes or low per-minute rates.

Bundled Minutes

Monthly fee includes a pool of minutes (e.g., 1,000 outbound minutes to US/Canada). Overage charged at standard rates. Predictable billing but may not suit international calling patterns.

Quality of Service (QoS) for SIP

Voice quality problems — jitter, latency, packet loss — are the most common SIP trunk issues. Prevent them:

  • Separate VoIP VLAN: Isolate voice traffic from data traffic on your network
  • QoS marking: Mark SIP and RTP packets with DSCP EF (Expedited Forwarding) priority
  • Firewall rules: Open UDP 5060 (SIP signaling) and UDP 10000-20000 (RTP media) to your provider's IP range
  • NAT traversal: Configure STUN or ensure your router handles SIP ALG correctly (often best to disable SIP ALG)

International SIP Trunking for Global Businesses

International businesses particularly benefit from SIP trunking because:

  • Originate calls from any country: A company in China can use a US SIP trunk to make calls appear from a +1 number
  • Wholesale international rates: Direct carrier connections provide wholesale per-minute rates, typically 70–80% cheaper than consumer VoIP services
  • Multi-country presence: Combine SIP trunk with DID numbers in multiple countries — one PBX, multiple local numbers globally

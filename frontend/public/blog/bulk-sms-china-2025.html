How to Send Bulk SMS to China in 2025: Complete Business Guide

📅 May 2025 ⏱ 12 min read 🏷 Bulk SMS · China · OTP

China has over 1.67 billion mobile subscribers — but sending SMS there is more complex than any other market. This guide covers everything: regulations, carrier routing, OTP delivery, and how to select a gateway that actually delivers.

Why Sending SMS to China Is Different

China's mobile telecommunications market is entirely controlled by three state-owned carriers: China Mobile (the world's largest carrier by subscribers), China Unicom, and China Telecom. Unlike Western markets where international SMS routes through standard SS7 interconnects, China requires explicit bilateral agreements between international gateways and these three carriers.

The result: most generic bulk SMS providers have poor or unreliable delivery to Chinese numbers (+86). Messages may be silently dropped, delayed by hours, or filtered by the carriers' spam-detection systems without any delivery receipt.

Key Fact: China's three carriers filter roughly 40–60% of inbound international SMS. Messages from unwhitelisted sender IDs, flagged content, or routes without carrier agreements are silently discarded — with no error returned to the sender.

Understanding China's SMS Regulations

The MIIT Framework

The Ministry of Industry and Information Technology (MIIT) regulates all SMS sent to Chinese numbers. Key requirements include:

  • Sender ID restrictions: Promotional SMS must use carrier-assigned numeric sender IDs. Alphanumeric sender IDs (like "MyBrand") are blocked on all three carriers.
  • Content filtering: Messages containing keywords related to gambling, financial products, real estate promotion, or political content are automatically filtered.
  • Opt-out compliance: Promotional messages must include an opt-out instruction (退订回复TD). Failure to include this risks carrier blacklisting.
  • Time restrictions: Carrier agreements typically prohibit sending promotional SMS between 22:00 and 08:00 China Standard Time (UTC+8).

ICP Registration and Content Approval

If you operate a service in China (with an ICP license), you can apply directly to carriers for SMS sending permissions. Foreign businesses without ICP registration must work through licensed international gateway providers who hold the necessary carrier agreements.

Choosing the Right SMS Gateway for China

What to Look For

Not all SMS gateways are equal when it comes to China delivery. Before selecting a provider, verify these critical factors:

  1. Direct carrier connections: Ask specifically whether the provider has direct bilateral agreements with China Mobile, China Unicom, and China Telecom — or whether they route through resellers and aggregators (which add latency and reduce reliability).
  2. Real delivery receipts: China carriers return actual DLR (Delivery Receipt) codes. A provider that shows all messages as "delivered" without DLR confirmation is hiding actual delivery failures.
  3. Content pre-screening: Good providers offer content validation before sending to prevent your messages from triggering keyword filters.
  4. Dedicated vs. shared routes: For OTP and transactional SMS, use dedicated routes. Shared routes are cheaper but a single bad actor using the same route can get you temporarily blacklisted.

Optimizing OTP Delivery to China

One-time password delivery is the most common use case for international businesses sending SMS to China — think user registration, login verification, and transaction confirmation. Here's how to maximize delivery rates:

1. Keep OTP Messages Short and Clean

Chinese carrier filters analyze message content. Optimal OTP format:

【YourApp】Your verification code is 847291. Valid for 10 minutes. Do not share this code.

Avoid: URLs, special characters, excessive punctuation, or content that resembles phishing messages.

2. Use 【Brand】 Prefix

The 【bracket notation】at the start of a message is a Chinese SMS convention that signals a legitimate business sender. Carriers and users both expect it — messages without it have lower trust scores.

3. Implement Fallback Mechanisms

Even with the best gateway, occasional delivery failures happen. Implement a fallback sequence:

  1. Primary SMS attempt (0 sec)
  2. If no DLR within 30 seconds → retry via alternate route
  3. If still no DLR within 90 seconds → offer voice OTP or email fallback

4. Monitor DLR Codes

China carrier DLR codes tell you exactly why a message failed:

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Using alphanumeric sender IDs — blocked by all China carriers. Use numeric IDs only.
  2. Sending via grey routes — temporary savings lead to permanent blacklisting.
  3. Ignoring time zones — sending at 3 AM CST will get messages filtered and damage your route reputation.
  4. Not validating numbers — send to invalid +86 numbers and your sender reputation suffers.
  5. Missing opt-out instructions on promotional messages — legal requirement under MIIT regulations.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I send marketing SMS to Chinese consumers without their consent?

No. Under China's Personal Information Protection Law (PIPL) and MIIT regulations, you must have explicit opt-in consent before sending promotional SMS. Violations can result in carrier blacklisting and legal penalties.

How long does SMS delivery take to China?

Premium direct-carrier routes: 3–15 seconds for OTP/transactional. Promotional messages may be queued and delivered within 1–5 minutes during peak hours.

What's the character limit for SMS to China?

Standard GSM-7 messages: 160 characters per segment. Messages in Chinese (Unicode/UCS-2): 70 characters per segment. Most gateways concatenate multi-part messages automatically.

Do I need a local entity in China to send SMS?

No. International businesses can send through licensed international gateway providers like Kaolach that hold the necessary carrier agreements. No local entity or ICP license required.
